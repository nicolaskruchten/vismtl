Critiquing Visualizations

  • Inspired by Edward Tufte, Junk Charts blog, WTFvis.net, Cartastrophe
  • 3 utilitarian lenses for looking at visualizations: as maps, as tools, as stories
  • (not as art, sorry)

Maps

  • The prototypical data visualizations
  • Represent spatial data spatially
  • Take something big/far away and make it small/close
  • Highlight certain aspects of reality

Critiquing Maps

  • Legible, accurate?
  • Appropriate projection/scale?
  • Consistency of colours and symbols, a legend?
  • Enables spatial reasoning?

Does this visualization allow me to reason spatially about the data?

Tools

  • Devices used to carry out a particular function
  • Let humans play to their strengths
  • "Use the right tool for the job"

Critiquing Tools

  • Clear intended purpose and user?
  • Adaptated for intended purpose and user?
  • Ergonomic?
  • Beneficially trade one type of difficulty for another?
  • Bad side-effects?

Does this visualization make it easier to accomplish the intended task?

Stories

  • (in the journalistic sense of the word)
  • Bring something to your attention
  • Teach or explain something
  • Convince you of something
  • Facts and context
  • Always biased, more or less transparently

Critiquing Stories

  • Deliver the intended message?
  • Lies, damned lies and statistics
  • Fair and balanced?
  • Clear about sources and biases?
  • Provide useful context?

Do I understand what this visualization is saying and do I believe it?

Key Questions

  • Can I look at this visualization as a map, a tool, or a story?
  • Does this visualization allow me to reason spatially about the data?
  • Does this visualization make it easier to accomplish the intended task?
  • Do I understand what this visualization is saying and do I believe it?
